# Sup 18421 Enterprise 429 Spike After Priority Routing Rollout

Source type: jira
Document ID: dsid_1980f45c3fb4455f943a2b030892ae90
Release: v1.0.0
Benchmark: EnterpriseRAG-Bench synthetic upstream data
Proxima Bank: unexpected spike in 429s after enterprise priority routing rollout (chat + embeddings)

## Issue summary
Customer (Proxima Bank) reports a sharp increase in HTTP 429 responses starting ~2026-01-20 09:10 PT on /v1/chat/completions and /v1/embeddings. They believe this correlates with Redwood’s recent “priority routing / capacity protection” changes.

## Impact
- Customer batch + online traffic intermittently fails with 429s, causing retry storms on their side.
- Reported elevated end-user latency due to retries; some requests fail after their max-retry budget.
- Customer concern: enterprise tier expected fewer 429s, not more.

## Environment
- Hosted API, prod
- Region: us-east
- Routes: /v1/chat/completions, /v1/embeddings
- Auth: enterprise API key (account: PROXIMA-ENT-014)

## What customer is seeing
- 429 response body includes Redwood reason codes (examples pasted by customer):
  - "reason": "overload_protection_admission"
  - "reason": "quota_exceeded" (less frequent)
- Response headers observed:
  - Retry-After: 1 (sometimes 2)
  - X-Redwood-Request-Id present
  - X-Redwood-Throttle-Reason: overload_protection_admission (most common)

## Customer notes
- They run bursty workloads: 10–30s spikes during market-open events.
- Client currently retries immediately with fixed 100ms delay and no jitter.

## Steps to reproduce (customer)
1) Send ~800–1200 RPS bursts to /v1/chat/completions with streaming disabled.
2) Concurrently send embedding bursts (~2k RPS) during the same window.
3) Observe 429 rates rising from <0.2% to 6–12% during bursts.

## Expected behavior
- Enterprise priority should protect them during fleet pressure; customer expects either stable acceptance or clearly defined, predictable throttling limits.

## Actual behavior
- Customer sees throttling during burst windows and requests clarification on:
  1) Why enterprise traffic is being rejected
  2) Whether the 429s are overload shedding vs quota
  3) Whether Redwood can grant a temporary burst exception / larger budget

## Initial support response (guidance provided)
- Confirmed that the new system returns distinct reason codes and customers should key their retry/backoff behavior on:
  - "quota_exceeded" (respect quota windows; reduce steady-state rate)
  - "overload_protection_admission" (transient overload; respect Retry-After; use exponential backoff + jitter)
- Recommended changes:
  - Use exponential backoff with full jitter; cap concurrency; do not retry immediately.
  - Honor Retry-After; treat 429 as a signal to shed load client-side.

## Investigation notes (internal)
Hypothesis: us-east protected-route capacity protection enabled for Proxima allowlisted accounts; per-route/per-tier admission control is active and may be gating bursts more aggressively than intended for this customer’s contractual traffic shape.

Checks performed:
- Correlated spike with protection annotations in hot-route dashboard (SRE): overload protection activated for route-group "chat" + "embeddings" during a concurrent regional pressure event.
- Confirmed most 429s for this account carry reason=overload_protection_admission (not quota).
- Verified no corresponding 5xx spike; p99 latency for enterprise class remained within SLO during event, indicating protections are working for fleet health but customer’s burst profile is being clipped.

Open questions
- What is Proxima’s contracted peak/burst behavior (RPS and token/s) for these routes?
- Is Proxima being routed to a stricter per-route budget due to route-group defaults?

## Requested outcome
- Explain semantics and provide an actionable plan.
- If eligible, grant a temporary burst exception with guardrails (time-boxed, monitored), or propose a dedicated capacity option.
