Usage-Based Pricing Legal Terms Playbook (Tokens, Retries, Caching, Credits, Disputes)

## Purpose
This playbook provides Redwood Inference’s approved legal guidance and clause library for **usage-based pricing** across Redwood API (Hosted) and related packaging. It is designed to:
- Ensure **consistent definitions** (tokens, requests, cache hits, retries) across MSAs, Order Forms, pricing pages, and Sales communications.
- Reduce **invoice disputes** by aligning contract language with metering behavior.
- Support **revenue recognition (RevRec)** and audit readiness by defining authoritative sources, dispute windows, and credit/refund mechanics.

This page is **restricted** because it contains clause language and internal positions.

## Scope
Applies to:
- Hosted API usage-based charges (tokens/requests/embeddings units)
- Metered add-ons where usage events are the basis of invoicing
- Dedicated and Private agreements only where they incorporate usage-based overages or burst (primary Dedicated meter definitions live elsewhere)

Out of scope:
- Full MSA templates (see Legal templates repository)
- Export-controlled or regulated country-specific addenda (escalate to Legal)

## Definitions (approved internal meanings)
The following definitions should be used as the “source definitions” when drafting or redlining contract language. If a customer demands alternate language, follow the escalation path below.

### 1) “Token”
**Token** means a discrete unit of text as processed by the applicable model’s tokenization scheme. Tokenization is model-dependent.

Important internal positions:
- Token counts are **model-specific** and may differ between models and versions.
- For chat-style endpoints, token counts may include system/developer messages, tool call payloads, and structured output formatting as applicable.
- We support (and prefer to contract around) **billed tokens** as the invoicing unit, rather than raw “input + output tokens,” because billed tokens reflects caching/discounting rules.

Recommended terminology:
- **Input Tokens / Prompt Tokens**: tokens attributable to the request context provided by the customer.
- **Output Tokens / Completion Tokens**: tokens generated by the model in response.
- **Billed Tokens**: the token quantity used for billing after applying billing rules (e.g., caching adjustments, promotional discounts, free-tier inclusion), as shown on the invoice and/or usage reports.

### 2) “Request”
A **Request** is an API call that is accepted by Redwood’s service and results in an attempt to process the call (including streaming requests). Do not tie “request” to “successful response” unless Product/Finance explicitly approves.

### 3) “Usage Report” / “Usage API”
**Usage Report** means Redwood’s reported usage as displayed in the Console, invoice detail, or usage export/API. Contract language should clarify which report is authoritative for billing.

Internal preference:
- For contracts: define that **Redwood’s invoice and supporting usage report** is the authoritative basis for fees, subject to the dispute process.

### 4) “Caching” / “Cache Hit”
Caching may reduce compute performed by Redwood. Contract language must avoid obligating Redwood to charge based on compute cost; instead, define how caching affects billed tokens.

Internal position:
- We may apply **cache-hit attribution** to reduce billed usage for eligible cached content, as described in product documentation.
- Caching behavior can change over time as the service is optimized; the contract should permit evolution while maintaining transparency via reports.

### 5) “Retry”
Retry can occur on:
- **Client side** (customer’s SDK/app retries)
- **Server side** (Redwood retries/fallback routing to preserve availability)

Internal positions:
- We aim to bill **once per logical request** where retries are transparently handled under the same idempotency key.
- If the customer does not use idempotency keys and submits duplicate requests, those may be billable.

## Drafting guidance (what to include in usage-based terms)
### Required building blocks
For any Order Form/SOW with usage-based fees, include:
1) Usage unit definition (tokens, embeddings units, requests, GPU-seconds if applicable)
2) Billing measurement and reporting source (invoice + usage report)
3) Caching and retries treatment (at least at a high level)
4) Dispute window + audit mechanics (reasonable; avoids open-ended re-billing risk)
5) Credits/refunds policy (when available; how applied)
6) Change management notice (especially if customer requests strict metering invariants)

### Optional but recommended
- Timezone for daily usage exports (default: UTC) if customer cares about day-level reporting
- Explicit acknowledgment that tokenization differs by model and may evolve with model upgrades

## Clause library (approved language)
Use the below clauses as starting points. Do not materially alter without Legal review.

### A. Usage unit (Tokens) — baseline clause
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Usage is measured in tokens processed by the applicable model. A token is a unit of text determined by the model’s tokenization scheme. Fees are calculated based on the number of billed tokens reported by Redwood for the applicable billing period. Token counts may vary by model and model version.”

**Negotiation notes:**
- If customer requests a fixed tokenization algorithm, respond that tokenization is model-dependent and tied to the model provider’s implementation.
- If customer requests billing purely on input+output tokens, steer to “billed tokens” and point to reporting transparency.

### B. Usage unit (Requests) — when included
**Approved clause (standard):**
“A request is an API call accepted by the service for processing. Requests that are duplicated by Customer (including due to Customer retries without an idempotency key) may be counted as separate requests.”

### C. Streaming responses — how to describe
**Approved clause (standard):**
“For streaming responses, usage is measured based on tokens processed and reported by Redwood for the request. If a streaming connection is interrupted, Redwood may report usage for tokens processed up to the point of interruption.”

**Negotiation notes:**
- Avoid promising that interrupted streams are always free; we can grant credits case-by-case.

### D. Tool/function calling and structured output
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Where the service supports tool/function calling or structured output, tokens attributable to tool call inputs/outputs and associated formatting may be included in token usage as reported by Redwood.”

### E. Caching treatment
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Redwood may employ caching and other optimization techniques. Usage reports may distinguish cached and non-cached usage. Redwood may apply billing adjustments (including reduced billed tokens) for eligible cached content as described in Redwood documentation. Optimization techniques may change over time.”

**Alternative clause (customer requests explicit fairness statement):**
“Redwood will not charge more than once for the same logical request submitted with the same idempotency key, and may reduce charges when caching is applied, as reflected in the usage report.”

### F. Retries and idempotency
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Customer is responsible for implementing idempotency keys for retryable requests. Redwood will use commercially reasonable efforts to avoid duplicate billing for server-side retries performed under the same logical request identifier. Customer-submitted duplicate requests may be billable.”

**Negotiation notes:**
- Do not guarantee “no double billing ever.” Instead: “commercially reasonable efforts” + dispute process.

### G. Measurement system and authoritative records
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Fees are based on Redwood’s measurement systems and usage records. Redwood will provide usage reporting via invoice detail, Console, or usage export/API. In the event of conflict, the invoice and supporting usage report for the billing period will control, subject to Customer’s dispute rights.”

### H. Dispute window (recommended default)
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Customer must notify Redwood in writing of any good-faith dispute regarding an invoice within thirty (30) days of the invoice date, describing the basis for the dispute in reasonable detail. If Customer does not dispute an invoice within this period, the invoice will be deemed accepted.”

**Fallback positions:**
- 45 days: acceptable for larger enterprise if requested.
- 60+ days: requires CFO (Laura Bennett) approval due to RevRec and cash collection risk.

### I. Credits and refunds (usage-based)
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Credits, if any, are applied to future invoices unless otherwise required by law. Except as expressly provided in the Agreement, fees are non-refundable.”

**Optional add-on (when SLA credits apply):**
“Service credits are Customer’s sole and exclusive remedy for any failure to meet a service level, and will be calculated and applied as described in the applicable SLA.”

### J. Meter changes and notice
**Approved clause (standard):**
“Redwood may update its measurement and reporting systems from time to time. Redwood will not materially increase charges due solely to a measurement change without providing prior notice and an opportunity to review supporting reporting for the change.”

**Internal guidance:**
- If the customer requests strict “no metering changes” language, escalate; this conflicts with ongoing hardening efforts (shadow-billing compare, meter v2) and can create operational risk.

## Redlines: common asks and recommended responses
### 1) “Tokens must match OpenAI exactly.”
**Position:** We support OpenAI-compatible endpoints, but tokenization/attribution can differ by model and implementation.
**Response:** Offer transparency: billed tokens reported; provide sample usage exports; consider adding a “reporting parity” exhibit if necessary (requires Product + Eng + Legal review).

### 2) “Do not charge for retries / failed requests.”
**Position:** If compute is performed, some usage may be reported.
**Response:** Use idempotency + commercially reasonable efforts clause. Offer a credits mechanism for verified platform-caused duplicate charges.

### 3) “Caching must always reduce price.”
**Position:** Caching may reduce billed tokens depending on eligibility and product policy.
**Response:** Use caching clause; avoid hard commitments. If needed for the deal, define a limited-term promotional adjustment with Finance sign-off.

### 4) “Dispute window must be 90 days.”
**Position:** Generally no.
**Response:** Offer 45 days; if deal size justifies, escalate to CFO/GC with RevRec impact assessment.

### 5) “Audit rights to inspect raw logs.”
**Position:** No direct customer access to raw multi-tenant logs.
**Response:** Provide usage exports, reconciliation support, and audit reports as appropriate; reference access-controls policy.

## Internal operational guardrails (contracting)
### Month-end close and late-arriving events
Finance operates a month-end close process with a usage data freeze window and controlled backfills.
- Contract language should not obligate real-time corrections for late events.
- Use the dispute clause and reconciliation process to handle exceptions.

### Backfills and corrections
If Redwood discovers a metering bug:
- Default approach is **forward-looking fixes** with audit trail.
- Retroactive invoice changes require Finance + Legal approval.

### Customer communications
If contract or procurement asks for a customer-facing explanation:
- Sales should use the approved FAQ language (see Sales Enablement page) and avoid promising exact token parity or “never bill on retries.”

## Deal desk checklist (for usage-based terms)
Before sending paper:
1) Confirm the usage unit(s) match the product packaging (tokens vs requests vs embeddings)
2) Confirm “billed tokens” language is used where available
3) Ensure retries/idempotency clause is included if customer is retrying or using streaming
4) Confirm dispute window is 30 days (or approved exception)
5) Confirm credits policy matches Finance policy and RevRec requirements
6) Link to usage reporting source and avoid promises of exact parity with other providers
