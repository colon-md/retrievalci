Optimization Suggestions: estimated savings does not match billing/usage totals (Arkadia Finance)

Customer reports that the Console > Optimize > Suggestions view shows an estimated savings that does not reconcile with their billing totals for the same time period.

Customer impact:
- Confusion / loss of trust in "estimated $/day" shown in Suggestions drawer.
- Customer is using this as input for internal finance review; needs a defensible reconciliation.

Customer statement (from ticket intake):
- "The Suggestions panel says caching will save ~$1,250/day for /v1/chat on Llama-3 70B. Our usage/billing only moved by ~$300/day after we enabled caching. Where is the rest of the savings?"

Environment:
- Org: Arkadia Finance (enterprise)
- Region: us-east
- Routes: /v1/chat (primary), /v1/embeddings (secondary)
- Models observed in screenshots: rw-llama-3-70b-instruct, rw-qwen2.5-72b-instruct
- Suggestions feature flag: enabled (beta)
- Customer claims caching was enabled on 2026-02-19 ~17:00 ET

Steps to reproduce (customer):
1) Navigate to Console > Optimize > Suggestions
2) Filter to Project "prod-core" and Route "/v1/chat"
3) Open top caching suggestion (stable ID visible in drawer)
4) Note "Estimated savings ($/day)" and "Assumptions"
5) Compare to Billing > Invoices / Usage export for same days

Root cause classification: Not a billing bug; a reconciliation/expectation gap + missing clarity in UI.

Contributing factors:
- Suggestions $/day is based on compute-cost attribution aggregates (GPU-sec + token pricing) and does not equal net invoiced totals (credits/commit discounts, platform fees, taxes).
- Estimator assumes full adoption for the scope shown; does not incorporate rollout percentage for A/B/canary in v0.
- Cache warm-up can make first-day realized savings lower than steady-state estimate.
- Time window mismatch: suggestion uses trailing 7d baseline + stale_as_of; customer compared to two UTC days and initially used local time.

Recommendation to Support/CS:
- When customers compare to billing, redirect to Console Usage Analytics (compute-cost) for apples-to-apples.
- Ask for rollout percentage and whether change was applied across all routes/projects.
- Ensure they compare a 48–72h window post-enable to avoid warm-up noise.

Product follow-ups (non-blocking):
- Promote assumption text in UI (100% adoption, compute-cost basis, exclude discounts/credits).
- Consider adding an optional field in suggestion payload for "adoption_factor" or a UI control to scale savings by rollout percentage (future).
- Add docs section on reconciliation: compute-cost vs invoice total.
