Procurement: Legal Risk Triage & Escalation SOP

Overview
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) describes Redwood Inference's end-to-end triage for procurement items that require legal review or cross-functional escalation. The goal is to reduce cycle time, ensure appropriate risk controls for contracting and billing, and provide clear ownership and SLA targets for each review tier.

Scope
- Applies to all vendor engagements, SOWs, license purchases, hardware leases (including GPU reservations), SaaS subscriptions, and professional services agreements initiated via Procurement.
- Excludes low-value purchases that follow the corporate purchasing card (P-card) policy (see Finance policy: /confluence/finance-and-legal/procurement/p-card-guidelines).

Definitions
1) Low-risk procurement: Purchase value <$25k, no data access, vendor uses standard market terms, no cross-border data transfer.
2) Medium-risk procurement: $25k–$250k, includes limited data access (non-sensitive), or requires limited bespoke terms (payment schedules, warranty clarifications).
3) High-risk procurement: >$250k, access to customer data or models, on-prem or network connectivity required, escrow/KMS requirements, change to indemnity/limitation clauses, or vendor requested data residency exceptions.
4) Contracting Owner: Procurement lead assigned to the purchase.
5) Legal Triage Owner: Commercial counsel assigned to initial review.

Decision Matrix (quick reference)
| Value band | Data access | Special clauses requested | Initial path | SLA (initial review) | Escalation
| --- | --- | --- | --- | ---: | --- |
| <$25k | None | Standard T&C | Procurement approval | 2 business days | Procurement Manager
| <$25k | Non-sensitive | Minor clarifications | Procurement + Legal FYI | 3 business days | Legal (if changes requested)
| $25k–$250k | Non-sensitive | Payment / warranty edits | Legal triage | 5 business days | Senior Counsel
| >$250k or any sensitive data | Any | Any bespoke confidentiality / KMS / escrow | Legal + Security + Finance | 10 business days | GC + Head of Procurement + Security

Procedure (step-by-step)
1. Procurement Request Intake
   a. Requestor opens a PR in the Procurement system (Jira/Workday) with required attachments: vendor quote, SOW, standard T&C, and vendor risk questionnaire.
   b. Procurement Owner tags the request with one of the risk bands and assigns Contracting Owner.
2. Automated Pre-checks (Procurement)
   a. Validate vendor is in the approved vendor list and has completed KYC.
   b. Run vendor risk scan (third-party risk tool) — attach report.
3. Legal Triage (Commercial Counsel)
   a. Counsel performs 3-screen review: (1) identify material exposures (indemnity, liability caps, warranty, IP, data access); (2) verify billing & rev-rec implications (see RevRec checklist below); (3) recommend standard redlines.
   b. Counsel applies template responses from the Clause Library for common items (data transfer addendum, limited indemnity, software license grant).
4. Cross-functional Review (when required)
   a. Security: Required for any network connectivity, data access, KMS/escrow, or on-prem deployments. Security lead must sign off within 3 business days of assignment.
   b. Finance: Required for contracts with atypical billing cadence, revenue-sharing, or subscription modifications; Finance must verify revenue recognition implications and billing trigger alignment.
   c. Product/Platform: Required for technical SOW accuracy and operational acceptance criteria for managed services.
5. Negotiation & Approval
   a. Procurement leads negotiations on non-legal commercial terms; legal negotiates legal language.
   b. For high-risk contracts, the final approval requires sign-off from Head of Procurement, Commercial Counsel, and Director of Finance.
6. Execution & Post-signature Controls
   a. Store executed contract in the contract repository (DocuSign + ContractDB) and tag with metadata (see Contract Metadata section).
   b. Trigger supplier onboarding checklists: finance onboarding (AP routing), security onboarding (vulnerability scan), and ops onboarding (service acceptance).

Contract Metadata (required fields stored in ContractDB)
- contract_id: unique Redwood identifier (e.g., RDW-CTR-2026-000123)
- vendor_legal_name
- purchasing_owner: "team/person"
- commercial_owner: "Procurement lead"
- legal_owner: "Commercial counsel"
- start_date / end_date / renewal_terms
- not_to_exceed_value / committed_spend_line_items
- billing_trigger: invoicing cadence and event that triggers rev-rec (see billing examples)
- data_classification_and_controls: none | non-sensitive | regulated | restricted
- kyc_status: complete | incomplete
- escrow_or_kms_required: yes | no
- confidentiality: internal | restricted | customer-only
- signed_copy_link: internal URL to storage

Revenue Recognition & Billing Checklist (Finance items to confirm before legal approval)
- Identify performance obligations and the billing trigger for each (e.g., access to API endpoints vs. delivery of physical hardware).
- Confirm whether contract changes the timing of revenue recognition (advance payments, non-refundable fees, milestone billing).
- Map invoicing cadence to subscription terms; attach billing examples: 
  Example 1: Monthly subscription — bill in arrears on 1st of following month; revenue recognized ratably over month.
  Example 2: Annual prepay — bill at execution; revenue recognized ratably over 12 months.
  Example 3: Hardware + installation SOW — hardware recognized at delivery; installation revenue recognized over acceptance period (SOW acceptance).

Service Level Targets for Reviews
- Procurement initial intake validation: 1 business day.
- Legal initial triage (see Decision Matrix): 2–10 business days depending on band.
- Security review when required: 3 business days.
- Complete negotiated contract to signature for medium-risk: target 15 business days; high-risk: target 30 business days.

Exception Handling & Emergency Procurement
- Emergency procurement (e.g., critical hardware replacement) may be approved by Head of Procurement + Head of Finance with retroactive legal review. Document the emergency rationale and complete a post-facto risk assessment within 5 business days.
- For multi-year commitments with budget implications, Procurement must coordinate with Budget Owners and FP&A to surface commitment in the next forecast cycle.

Template Clauses (common redlines and recommended language)
1) Limitation of Liability (recommended cap): "Except for willful misconduct or breach of confidentiality, liability shall not exceed the greater of (i) USD 1,000,000 or (ii) the fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim."
2) Data Processing Addendum (DPA) pointer: "Attach DPA described in Appendix A; vendor must support EU SCCs and provide KMS integration details for customer-managed keys where required."
3) Escrow/KMS: "For on-prem or model-hosting arrangements where customer datasets or model weights may be subject to e-discovery or regulatory requests, vendor must provide an escrow arrangement or KMS integration documented in Schedule 3."

Audit & Reporting Requirements
- Quarterly procurement compliance report to Finance and Legal: list of executed contracts > $100k and any deviations from SOP.
- Monthly aging report of contracts in negotiation (by risk band).
- Post-mortem on all high-risk negotiations that exceeded 30 business days: root cause and action items.

Appendix A: Sample Legal Redline Checklist (for counsel)
- Verify IP ownership and license grants.
- Check indemnity and carve-outs for open-source dependencies.
- Confirm limitation of liability and insurance obligations.
- Ensure confidentiality duration and return/destruction procedures.
- Confirm termination for convenience and transition assistance obligations.
